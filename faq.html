Frequently Asked Questions

1. What is MobControl?

MobControl is a system that turns your smartphone into a fully functional game controller for PC games. It includes an Android mobile app (for creating layouts and sending inputs) and a Windows desktop app (for input mapping and managing connections).

2. What are the system requirements to use MobControl?

Mobile App (Android): Android 6.0+ (ARM64), 500MB+ storage, rear camera for QR pairing, WiFi connection.

Desktop App (Windows): Windows 10/11, .NET 8 SDK, Quad-core CPU, 16GB RAM recommended.

3. How do I connect my phone to the desktop application?

You can connect in two ways:

QR Pairing: Open the app → Settings → “Connect to PC - Scan QR Code” → Scan the QR displayed on the desktop app.

Manual Input: Enter the IP address, port, and token shown on the desktop app into the mobile app.

4. Can multiple players use MobControl at the same time?

Yes. MobControl supports multiple mobile devices connected simultaneously. Each phone is treated as a separate input stream, allowing local multiplayer gameplay.

5. What should I do if pairing fails?

Try the following troubleshooting steps:

> Ensure both devices are connected to the same WiFi/LAN.

> Verify that the desktop firewall allows port 8181 connections.

> Clean your camera lens and scan again if QR code is not recognised.

> Restart the app and check permissions for camera and network.

6. Does MobControl work with all games?

MobControl translates mobile inputs into standard PC inputs (keyboard/gamepad). It works with most PC and open-source games, but some games with strict anti-cheat systems may block simulated input.

7. Do I need the internet to use MobControl?

No, MobControl works entirely over local networks (LAN). An internet connection is not required once the apps are installed.
